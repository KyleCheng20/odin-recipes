Spam Fried Rice

Description

Spam fried rice is a quick, flavorful stir-fry that transforms simple ingredients into a satisfying meal. It typically features diced Spam that’s pan-fried until crispy, then tossed with cooked rice, scrambled eggs, vegetables like peas, carrots, or green onions, and seasoned with soy sauce or other savory sauces. The salty, savory richness of Spam pairs perfectly with the fluffy rice and fresh vegetables, making it both comforting and versatile. Popular in Hawaii and across parts of Asia, it's a budget-friendly, easy-to-make dish that works well as a main course or a side.

Ingredients

  • Spam
  • Cooked White Rice
  • Shallot or Onion
  • Mixed Vegetables
  • Green Onion
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Soy Sauce & Oyster Sauce
  • Sesame Oil
  • Sugar, Salt, and Pepper
  • Vegetable Oil

Steps

  1. Prep everything. Dice the Spam, chop your veggies, beat the eggs, and make sure the rice is cooled and ready.
  2. Fry diced Spam in a hot wok or skillet until golden and crispy. Set aside.
  3. Sauté aromatics. Add oil, sauté shallots until fragrant and transparent.
  4. Add mixed vegetables. Stir-fry the vegetables until just tender.
  5. Scramble eggs. Push veggies to the side, scramble the eggs in the same pan.
  6. Add rice & season it up. Toss in the rice and break up clumps. Add butter, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, sugar, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  7. Finish with Spam. Add the Spam back in. Let it crisp at the bottom for 2 minutes, stir, and repeat. Top with green onions, extra sesame oil, or sriracha if you're feeling spicy.
