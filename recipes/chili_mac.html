Chili Mac

Description

Chili mac is a hearty comfort food that combines elements of chili and macaroni and cheese into a single dish. It typically features elbow macaroni mixed with a savory, seasoned chili made from ground beef (or turkey), beans, tomatoes, and spices like chili powder, cumin, and paprika. Some versions also include cheese, either stirred in for creaminess or melted on top for extra richness. The result is a filling, flavorful meal that's both budget-friendly and easy to make, often served as a family dinner, potluck dish, or quick weeknight option.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 8 oz cans tomato sauce
  • 2 15 oz cans black beans (or pinto beans), drained
  • 1 14.5 oz can petite diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 32 oz chicken broth
  • 3 cups small macaroni noodles
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese, plus more for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat a large Dutch oven or pot over medium-high heat and coat it with a couple of swirls of olive oil.
  2. Add the ground beef, onion, and minced garlic, and cook until the beef is browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, and cook for about 30 seconds.
  4. Add the tomato sauce, diced tomatoes, drained beans, and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Add the macaroni noodles and continue to cook, stirring regularly, until the noodles are al dente, about 10 minutes.
  6. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the shredded cheese until melted and well combined.
  7. Serve the Chili Mac and Cheese topped with sour cream, sliced avocado, fresh parsley, and extra shredded cheese, if desired.
